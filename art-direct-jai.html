Jai Style

Background

In the summer of 2016, Karen was in the midst of a pivotal rebrand for her jewelry brand, Jai Style. At the time, the rebrand was necessary because the look and feel of the brand no longer reflected the message and style that Karen had shifted to since the brand’s inception in 2014. Karen approached me to help create a new set of photos for her website and social media profiles which reflected the core of the brand, while embracing a lighter, fresher take on the visual art direction to better communicate the brand.

How might we create a visual direction which reflects the Jai Style brand, while depicting the unique story of its maker?

Approach

We began by creating a moodboard in order to establish the art direction of the brand. The result was to have a mix of textures and visuals which exude movement, freedom, and exploration, yet maintained an underlying sense of calm.

Taking a closer look at the colour palette of the brand, we investigated the interplay of light and shadow, on an earthy, pastel colour palette. This colour palette is also a more subtle take on the bright, vibrant colours found in Karen’s photos taken while living in Thailand and various parts of Asia.

Solution

Over the past two years, we’ve worked together to establish a flexible content strategy that suits her needs, along with original photography, which encapsulates her story and travels while ultimately showcasing her handmade jewelry collection.

Reflection

With every shoot, Karen and I have established a process that has enough structure to ensure we capture what she needs, yet allows room for spontaneity in the styling of our shoots. The biggest challenge is trying to find new, creative ways to capture the jewelry pieces.

Between Karen, myself, and our model, Lindsay, we’ve managed to create an environment that feels creatively organic and unforced. We’ll often bounce ideas off each other over the course of the day, while taking breaks to pour of Pinterest, eat light snacks, and sip on tea.
