In-Studio with Mischa Pertsev

February 2018

As a designer, I constantly search for inspiration in the world around me. I use photography as my primary method to capture the world around me. For me, I am inspired by the methods that other artists and designers use to stay inspired. By learning about other artist’s processes and sources of inspiration, I am able to further push myself creatively. This mentality was the driving force behind the purpose of coordinating studio shoots with local artists.

Meet Mischa

I met Mischa Pertsev while at the Eastside Culture Crawl back in November 2016. Since then, we have helped gotten to know each other by collaborating on a few projects.

For this shoot, Mischa invited me to to his studio as he taught a few of his regular students. His approach to ceramics is constantly iterative, focussing on multiple ongoing projects rather than a solo piece. The studio is bustling as Mischa offers feedback to students, soft music plays in the background, a kettle of tea steeps in the corner. The combination of refined sculptures amongst the unfinished clay explorations provide a beautiful juxtaposition which contributes to the creative pulse of Mischa’s studio.
